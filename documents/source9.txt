It honestly depends on your lifestyle schedule and any scholarships. Everyone’s situation is different. 

Scholarship wise UV is the best, also if you have class at a variety of times and want to stop by your apartment in between classes. 

Northside would be good for if you have all your classes back to back then want to leave for the day and not come back. It is a little more expensive since as of class of 22, HOUSING STIPENDS ARENT APPLICABLE FOR NORTHSIDE. It has furnished and unfurnished options, a wider variety, and an ever so slightly better quality than UV. It’s also good for partying (since they have more lax rules than UV) and access to the restaurants below. _Northside has year long leases vs school year long leases for on campus housing and there is no buyback system_.


Canyon creek is good if you have scholarship as well and no money/means of moving furniture. It has better parking where it is than UV, but it is quite a walk to campus proper so not good for weird schedules.

*If you are using money from a 529 account you can use it for UV and CC unhindered. **If you are using it for northside it cannot be a room that costs more than $944.50 a month (based upon my calculations following the rules of a Texas used non prepaid 529 account).***

I’d do your research on the websites instead of asking personal anecdotes because 1) the quality of all is bad and 2) it genuinely depends on your situation.

In my opinion since I’m not on scholarship UV is the way to go. We have had to do multiple work order requests, but it hasn’t been a bad experience. I’ll compare my experiences here:

CCHN
-mold problem 
-fire alarms all the time
-they are more secure 
-furnished
-rooms a lot smaller
-you use your comet card to enter the room

UV
-multiple work orders needed when we moved in
-rooms a lot bigger, has ceiling fan, etc
-I have an actual closet not a hole in the wall
-cheaper
-usually laundry machines in apartment
-patio on some.
-closer to everything
-parking is better: last semester the parking was so much farther at CCHS than at Uv
-you use an actual key to enter your room

Summary: Canyon Creek is dorms 2.0 and UV is like an actual apartment, so it really depend on what you want.
